PESTO

DESCRIPTION

Pesto is a vibrant green, fresh-tasting sauce that originated in Genoa, Italy. It’s traditionally made with a combination of fresh basil, Parmesan cheese, olive oil, garlic, and pine nuts. The name “pesto” comes from the Italian word “pestare,” which means “to crush.” Though modern recipes (such as this one) rely on blenders or food processors, the ingredients for pesto are traditionally crushed together using a mortar and pestle.


INGREDIENTS

  • 3 cups packed fresh basil leaves
  • ¾ cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • ½ cup olive oil
  • ¼ cup pine nuts
  • 4 cloves garlic, or to taste
  • 2 teaspoons lemon juice, or more to taste (optional)

STEPS

  1. Combine basil, Parmesan cheese, olive oil, pine nuts, and garlic in the bowl of a food processor or blender. Blend to a smooth paste. Add lemon juice, if desired, and quickly pulse to combine.
